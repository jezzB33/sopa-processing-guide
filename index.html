Simplified SOPA Processing Standards for Small-Scale Farmers

1. Do You Need Certification for Processing?

You need certification if you:

  • Process or prepack organic food
  • Repack or relabel out of consumer sight
  • Blend or compound animal feeds
  • Store or clean bulk crops
  • Slaughter livestock
  • Import/export organic products
  • Use non-organic materials in processing

No certification needed if you:

  • Sell sealed packs directly to consumers
  • Transport bulk goods (except milk)
  • Handle salt or non-food items
Reference: Section 5.3 SOPA Processing Standards

2. On-Farm Processing Included on Your Certificate

Allowed when:

  • Less than 10% of ingredients are bought-in
  • The farm is fully organic
  • Only simple processing is used

Examples: cleaning, chopping, packing, milling grain, mixing muesli

Reference: Section 5.4 SOPA Processing Standards

3. When You Need a Separate Processing Certificate

Required if:

  • More than 10% ingredients are bought-in
  • You process organic & non-organic items
  • Complex processes are used (e.g. baking, fermentation)
Reference: Section 5.5 SOPA Processing Standards

4. Certification and Compliance Process

  • Audit by OF&G (Scotland) or subcontractor
  • Feedback and required actions
  • Status from “Compliant” to “Suspended”
  • Lack of action raises your compliance category
Reference: Section 5.6 SOPA Processing Standards

5. Labelling Requirements

  • 95% or more organic content = “Organic” label allowed
  • Label must include:
    • Clear organic ingredient ID
    • Certifier code (e.g., GB-ORG-17)
    • EU Organic Logo (if eligible)
  • <95% organic: “organic” allowed only in ingredient list
Reference: Section 6.1 SOPA Processing Standards
